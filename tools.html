Research Tools

The VAMOS group develops interactive tools based on radiative transfer theory to support quantitative remote sensing research and education. These tools allow users to explore soil, leaf, and canopy optical properties through web-based simulations, facilitating a deeper understanding of vegetation radiative processes and model behavior.

🌿 SoilRTM

Interactive simulation of soil reflectance using radiative transfer models for analyzing soil optical properties.

Open Tool

🌱 LeafRTM

Leaf radiative transfer model for exploring the relationship between leaf biochemical parameters and spectral reflectance.

Open Tool

🌳 CanopyRTM

Canopy-level radiative transfer simulations linking vegetation structure and optical signals observed by remote sensing.

Open Tool

These tools are designed for researchers, students, and practitioners interested in vegetation remote sensing and radiative transfer modeling. They provide intuitive interfaces for exploring how structural and biochemical parameters influence spectral signals from soil, leaves, and plant canopies.

The tools are continuously being improved and expanded to support education, algorithm development, and scientific exploration in quantitative remote sensing.
